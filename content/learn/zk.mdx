## From Password Boxes to Blockchains

The same principle applies to moneyâ€”just swap "password" for "private key" and "random number" for "transaction details".

**On Bitcoin/Ethereum:**
- "I'm sending you $100"
- Everyone sees: sender, receiver, amount, your entire balance history
- It's like publishing your bank statement on a billboard

**On Aleo with Zero-Knowledge:**
- "I'm proving I CAN send you $100"
- Everyone sees: âœ“ transaction verified
- Nobody sees: your balance, the amount, who you're paying, your history

The network knows the transaction is mathematically valid. But your financial privacy is preserved.

## Why This Changes Everything

With zero-knowledge proofs, you can build applications that are:

- **Private** â€” Your data stays yours
- **Compliant** â€” You can prove you followed the rules without exposing details
- **Verifiable** â€” Everyone can check the proof is valid

You can prove:

- You're eligible for a service (without revealing your identity)
- A transaction is valid (without exposing the amounts)
