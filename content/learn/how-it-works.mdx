# Let's Build a Private Payment

Forget the theory for a second. Let's walk through what actually happens when you send money on Aleo.

**Scenario:** You want to send 50 ALEO tokens to your friend.

## Step 1: Your Secret Records

On Aleo, your tokens aren't just a number in a database. They're **encrypted records** that only you can see.

Your wallet might show:
```
Balance: 100 ALEO
```

But on the blockchain, it's stored as an encrypted record:
```
Record #4582 (encrypted)
Owner: [your address]
Amount: [encrypted: only you can decrypt]
```

Nobody else can see your balance. Not even the network validators.

## The Key Insight

Traditional blockchain: **Do everything publicly on-chain**
- Everyone sees everything
- Network processes every transaction
- Slow and exposed

Aleo: **Compute privately off-chain, prove publicly on-chain**
- You compute locally
- Network only verifies proofs
- Fast and private

It's like showing your work vs. showing the answer. Aleo shows the answer (proof) without showing the work (your private data).

<Callout type="success" title="The Breakthrough">
  By moving computation off-chain and verification on-chain, Aleo achieves both privacy AND scalability. Traditional blockchains can't do this.
</Callout>

## Why This Matters

This architecture enables things that were impossible before:

**Private Payments**
- Companies can do payroll on-chain
- Banks can use blockchain for customer accounts
- You can transact without exposing your finances

**Scalability**
- Your computer does the hard work
- The network only checks tiny proofs
- No congestion, low fees

**Programmability**  
- Write smart contracts in Leo
- Build any private app you can imagine
- Full blockchain capabilities with privacy
